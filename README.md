# Classification-and-Forecasting-of-Water-Stress-in-Tomato-Plants-using-Bioristor-Data
Water stress and in particular drought are some of the most significant factors affecting plant growth, food production, and thus food security. Furthermore, the possibility to predict and shape irrigation on real plant demands is priceless. The objective of this study is to characterize, classify, and forecast water stress in tomato plants by means of in vivo real time data obtained through a novel sensor, named barrister, and of different artificial intelligence models. First of all, we have applied classification models, namely Decision Trees and Random Forest, to try to distinguish four different stress statuses of tomato plants. Then, we have predicted, through the help of recurrent neural networks, the future status of a plant when considering both a binary (water stressed and not water stressed) and a four-status scenario. The obtained results are very good in terms of accuracy, precision, recall, F-measure, and of the resulting confusion matrices, and they suggest that the considered novel data and features coming from the barrister, together with the used machine and deep learning models, can be successfully applied to real-world on-the-field smart irrigation scenarios in the future.

The main objectives are: 
-    To collect and analyze real-time in vivo data from tomato plants using the bioristor sensor for monitoring physiological      responses related to water stress.
-    To preprocess and extract meaningful features from the sensor-generated time-series data for effective model training.
-    To develop classification models such as decision tree and random forest to accurately identify and distinguish four different water stress levels in tomato plants.
-    To build predictive models using recurrent neural networks (rnns) to forecast future plant stress conditions.
-    To implement both binary (stressed vs. non-stressed) and multi-class (four stress levels) prediction approaches for comprehensive analysis.
-    To evaluate model performance using accuracy, precision, recall, f1-score, and confusion matrix.
-    To support smart irrigation decision-making by enabling early detection and prediction of plant water stress.
-    To promote sustainable agriculture practices by optimizing water usage and improving crop productivity through ai-driven solutions.

The system provides several important features that enable efficient and accurate real-time monitoring and prediction of water stress in tomato plants.
-Real-time collection of plant physiological data using bioristor sensors. 
-Application of machine learning algorithms such as decision tree and random forest for stress classification. 
-Use of deep learning models like lstm for forecasting future water stress conditions. 
-Data preprocessing including cleaning, normalization, and feature extraction. 
-Support for both binary and multi-class classification of plant stress levels. 
-Integration with smart irrigation systems for automated decision-making. 
-High scalability to handle large volumes of sensor data. 
-Low latency enabling quick prediction and response for irrigation control.
